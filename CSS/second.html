A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. Formed from the collapse of massive stars, black holes have an event horizon, a point of no return, and a singularity, a center of infinite density. They distort spacetime, bending light and emitting radiation, and come in various sizes, from stellar to supermassive. Despite their elusive nature, black holes are supported by observational evidence from X-rays, radio waves, and gravitational waves.
